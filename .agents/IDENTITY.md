# Coco — Brand Identity & Philosophy

This document is for AI agents and developers working on the Coco codebase.
It defines the soul of the project: why it exists, what it feels like, and how to talk about it.

---

## Name Origin

"Coco" carries a dual meaning:

1. **Coco = Cabeza (head)** — In Latin American Spanish slang, "coco" means head.
   It's where thoughts are born, where ideas flash before they vanish. The app lives
   right at that moment: the instant between thinking something and losing it.

2. **Coco = Memory** — The Disney/Pixar movie *Coco* is a story about remembering,
   about not letting what matters fade away. That same spirit drives the app:
   capture it now so you never forget.

---

## The Coco

The brown animated arch at the bottom of the screen **is** the Coco. It's the heart
of the app — the place where you write.

- It has a warm gradient (dark brown tones, `CocoArchTop` → `CocoArchBottom`).
- A gentle wave animation runs along its top edge (two sine waves, 7-second cycle).
- It sits above the keyboard, always ready, always inviting.

When someone says "the Coco", they mean this arch. It's both a visual identity
and a functional surface.

---

## Notes (Notas)

We call entries **notes** (*notas*), not ideas. This is intentional:

- A note can be a thought, a reminder, a to-do, a name, a quote, a grocery item.
- "Ideas" sounds too specific and creative. "Notes" is broader and more honest.
- The app captures anything you don't want to forget — that's it.

---

## Design Philosophy

### Zero Friction
Open → keyboard ready → type → done. No splash delays, no onboarding walls,
no navigation to reach the input. The first thing you see is a place to write.

### Warm & Organic
- Brown tones, cream backgrounds, soft pastels.
- No sharp edges anywhere — everything is rounded, soft, bouncy.
- Neumorphic surfaces with subtle light/shadow for depth.
- Spring physics for all animations (no linear or abrupt curves).

### Premium Without Complexity
The app should feel high-quality and polished without being heavy or feature-bloated.
Every interaction has haptic feedback. Every transition has physics. Every surface
has intention. But the user never has to learn anything — it just works.

## Target User

Anyone who has fleeting thoughts and needs to capture them before they vanish.
Not power users managing complex projects. Not writers drafting long documents.
Just people who think of something and need it saved — *ahora mismo*.
